Salman Khan

A powerhouse in Bollywood, known for his charisma and contributions to Indian cinema and society.

Here's a time line of Salman Khan's life:

  • 1965 - Born on December 27 in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, India.
  • 1988 - Makes his acting debut in the film "Biwi Ho To Aisi" as a supporting actor
  • 1989 - Plays the lead role for the first time in "Maine Pyar Kiya," which becomes a blockbuster hit and catapults him to stardom.
  • 1990 - Receives the Filmfare Award for Best Male Debut for his performance in "Maine Pyar Kiya."
  • 1994 - Stars in "Hum Aapke Hain Koun..!", which becomes one of the highest-grossing Indian films of all time.
  • 1998 - Appears in "Pyaar Kiya To Darna Kya" and "Jab Pyaar Kisise Hota Hai," continuing his successful streak at the box office.
  • 2003 - Acts in "Tere Naam," a romantic drama that receives critical acclaim.
  • 2004 - Establishes the charitable organization "Being Human" to support education and healthcare initiatives for the underprivileged.
  • 2007 - Stars in "Partner," another major commercial success.
  • 2010 - Hosts the reality TV show "Bigg Boss," which becomes immensely popular in India. Features in "Dabangg," an action film that becomes a major blockbuster and establishes the "Dabangg" franchise.
  • 2011 - Acts in "Bodyguard," which sets new box office records.
  • 2012 - Launches the production company "Salman Khan Films."
  • 2015 - Stars in "Bajrangi Bhaijaan" which receives critical acclaim and becomes one of the highest-grossing Indian films.
  • 2016 - Features in "Sultan" a sports drama that earns widespread praise for his performance.
  • 2017 - Launches the "Being Human" e-cycles initiative to promote eco-friendly transportation.
  • 2020 - Launches the "Being Haangryy" food venture to provide meals to frontline workers during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • 2021 - Continues to be actively involved in philanthropy through various initiatives under the "Being Human" foundation, focusing on healthcare, education, and livelihood support for disadvantaged communities.

Salman Khan's dedication to social causes through Being Human has made a significant impact, touching the lives of many in India. His charitable contributions alongside his successful film career have cemented his legacy as not just a Bollywood superstar but also a humanitarian committed to making a positive difference in society.
